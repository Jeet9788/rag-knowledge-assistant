# Northwind Labs — Engineering FAQ

## Getting started
New hires request repository access through the internal service portal; access is
granted by your team lead once your accounts are provisioned. If you are blocked on
access during your first week, raise it with your onboarding buddy rather than waiting.

## Where to ask questions
Use the team channel for anything that might help others, and reserve direct messages for
private matters. There are no bad questions during your first month; asking early is
faster than guessing.

## Deployments
Services deploy continuously. Small changes go out several times a day once they pass
review and continuous integration. There is no fixed release day; shipping in small
increments is preferred over large batched releases.

## Working with other teams
When your change depends on another team, open a request early and describe the interface
you need. Cross-team interfaces are agreed in writing before implementation so both sides
can build in parallel.

## Remote collaboration tools
Distributed teams pair using shared editors and video. Recordings of design discussions
are stored in the team space so people in other timezones can catch up asynchronously.

## Meetings
Standups are short and focused on blockers. Design discussions are scheduled separately
and have a written agenda. A meeting without an agenda can be declined.

## Documentation
Every service has a short README describing how to run it locally and who owns it. If you
find documentation that is wrong, fixing it is always in scope.
